The Scope

At Imperio Tech, I was tasked with manufacturing high-precision shaft and gear components for industrial machinery. The primary challenge was maintaining tight geometric tolerances (concentricity and runout) on long turned parts while minimizing cycle time.

Execution & Process

1. Setup & Workholding

Rigidity is key to precision. I utilized soft jaws bored to the exact diameter of the workpiece to prevent marring and vibration. I indicated the part to within 0.005mm TIR (Total Indicator Runout) before starting the cycle.

2. Programming & Optimization

I reviewed the generated G-code and manually optimized the toolpaths. By adjusting the G96 (Constant Surface Speed) and feed rates for the finishing pass, I achieved the required Ra 0.8 surface finish without needing secondary grinding operations.

3. Inspection & Quality Control

Quality is verified at the machine, not just the inspection room. I used digital micrometers and bore gauges to verify critical dimensions immediately after the finishing pass. Tool wear offsets were adjusted in real-time to keep the process centered within the tolerance band.

Final Outcome

The optimized process reduced cycle time by 15% and achieved a 99.8% acceptance rate. The components passed all CMM (Coordinate Measuring Machine) validations and were delivered on schedule.
